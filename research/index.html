At SMC we are passionate about research - researching how and why learning mindfulness can be helpful. Most of our research is about looking at the potential benefits of mindfulness-based interventions for groups of people where there isn't already good research evidence. Ultimately, the aim of our research is to improve access to effective mindfulness-based interventions to a wider group of people.

We have been researching mindfulness-based interventions for people experiencing a range of mental health difficulties including obsessive-compulsive difficulties, depression and distressing voices. Some of our research focuses on working age adults but we have also been doing research involving young people and older adults. 

We are also involved in evaluating the potential benefits of self-help mindfulness interventions. Our mindfulness research is therefore aimed at potentially increasing access to evidence-based mindfulness-based interventions for those people who otherwise may not have access.
